Sport Executive Officer Update - Luke Thomson
With the winter rain and cold slowly taking a hold on Loughborough you might think everything is getting a little miserable, but with a number of AU Events coming up, we’ll hopefully keep you entertained. Coming up we have a number of Big Matches: firstly, our Men’s Basketball team take on Northumbria on the 30th November, and this is closely followed by Fight Night on December 3rd where the Loughborough Kickboxing team take on Edinburgh. Finally, we are going out to advert for a new Spectatorship Officer on the AU Exec. Trampolining Competition: Loughborough students trampolining had a fantastic event last week at their competition where Loughborough were the overall winners of the event! Over the course of the 2 day competition, 12 Loughborough students medalled; in 6 of the events in the competition Loughborough placed first. Kimberley Rayment in the Advanced individual trampoline event, Alex jefferson in the Elite individual trampoline event and Becca Bissetr and Esther Hinchley came first in the Lower Synchro double trampoline event; there were so many more successes of the day! Loughborough students trampolining has made us proud!
AU takes on Hey Ewe: On Wednesday 2nd & 9th of November we invited 31 of our AU clubs to descend on the Students’ Union and take part in a mix of activities that would test them physically and mentally. Challenges ranged from the agility based Batak Machine and strength based partner squats, all the way to the on stage limbo and pre-event fancy dress competition. From these two preliminaries only the best clubs would outshine the rest and work their way to a place in the final. And so… After a long and manic fortnight we are happy to announce the finalists for AU Takeover Hey Ewe 2016/17!
Basketball: Big Match: Loughborough Basketball will be taking on Northumbria in what is set to be one of the BIGGEST games of the season.
Table Tennis Event: Arguably the biggest event of the year for any BUCS table tennis player, the BUCS Table Tennis Individuals Championships is being held on 26th/27th November at University of Nottingham. Loughborough will be represented in all five main events throughout the weekend; a total of 15 players will be representing Loughborough in a series of events throughout the Championship. Lois Peake, seeded fifth in Women’s Singles, is extremely looking forward to the weekend: “I am really excited about this year's BUCS, particularly as I was unable to play last year due to injury. With the determination of all our players and the team spirit possess I think we can get big unexpected wins!”. For others, this will be the biggest event they have ever competed in and a good opportunity to watch some amazing table tennis. We wish all the best for the Loughborough squad and hope to gain some outstanding results from the weekend.
